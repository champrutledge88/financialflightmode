# FFM Scorecard Public Explanation Copy v1

## Short Result Explanation

Your Financial Flight Score reflects five core signals:

1. Cash flow
2. Savings system
3. Emergency runway
4. Debt load
5. Wealth fuel

This score is not a judgment. It is a cockpit check.

The goal is simple:

Know where you stand, understand the main signal, and take the next right move.

## Signal Copy

### Cash Flow

**Strong:** You have money remaining after monthly expenses.

**Building:** Your income covers the month, but the margin is tight.

**Needs Attention:** Your monthly outflow may be higher than your income.

### Emergency Runway

**Strong:** You have meaningful savings available for unexpected expenses.

**Building:** You have started building a buffer, but it needs more fuel.

**Low:** One unexpected expense could create pressure.

### Debt Load

**Low:** Debt pressure is not currently dominating your flight plan.

**Moderate:** Debt is manageable, but it still needs a clear strategy.

**Elevated:** Debt pressure may be limiting your cash flow and flexibility.

### Savings System

**On Course:** You are consistently setting money aside.

**Building:** You have started saving, but the habit needs consistency.

**Needs Attention:** Savings needs a clear role in your monthly plan.

## Status Copy

### Pre-Flight

You are not behind. You are getting clear.

Best next move:

Set up your monthly budget cockpit and identify where money is drifting.

### On Approach

You have some control, but a few signals need attention.

Best next move:

Protect cash flow, build emergency runway, and reduce one pressure point.

### Cruise Control

You have solid momentum.

Best next move:

Stay consistent, automate the right habits, and keep reviewing your numbers.

### Flight Mode

Your cockpit is showing strong control.

Best next move:

Keep the system running and look for ways to increase income, investing, and ownership.
